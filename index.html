PATH Townhalls

What is a
PATH Townhall?

A PATH Townhall is an interactive event where principals, teachers, and education stakeholders experience how PATH works in practice.

Townhalls are designed for decision makers who want more from education, not just be settled with the ‘best’ of the current. A PATH Townhall is an opportunity to get a hands-on introduction to a vastly different pedagogy, showing exactly how classrooms transform when this research-based program is implemented.

At a PATH Townhall, you don’t just hear about the program, you see it in action and take part yourself. Through live demonstrations, you’ll experience how this pedagogy:

Faster, joyful learning

Children grasp concepts quickly and with excitement

21st century skills

Built naturally into every lesson

Learning over teaching

Classrooms aligned with NEP 2020

Trust and support

Wins parent’s appreciation and teacher buy in

On the job growth

Teachers learn continuously in class, not just in workshops

Strong foundations

Fluency in English and languages, mastery in math

Build in feedback

Ongoing assessment improves learning for all

Clear insights

Trainers explain and demonstrate the approach

Practical next steps

Guidance on adopting PATH in your school

Peer network

Connect with schools and leaders exploring PATH

Online Townhalls

Hosted by the PATH team, these online interactive sessions make it possible for schools anywhere to join remotely and get the full experience.

In-School Townhalls

Some schools open their campus as the venue for a PATH Townhall. The PATH team runs the full session there, while invitations are jointly shared by GETI and the host school with principals and teachers in the city and nearby areas. Invitations go out through email, WhatsApp, local Sahodaya networks, and other channels, and always feature the host school’s name, logo, and location.
